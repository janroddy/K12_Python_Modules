  Molarity exercises   

  1. How many grams of potassium carbonate are needed to make 200 mL of a 2.5 M solution?
  2. 100.0 g of 4.0°C water is heated until its temperature is 37°C. If the specific heat of water is 4.18 J/g°C, calculate the amount of heat energy needed to cause this rise in temperature.
  3. A 15.75-g piece of iron absorbs 1086.75 joules of heat energy and its temperature changes from 25°C to 175°C. Calculate the specific heat capacity of iron.
  4. Calculate the specific heat capacity of a piece of wood if 1500.0 g of the wood absorbs 6.75×104 joules of heat and its temperature changes from 32°C to 57°C.
  5. 25.0 g of mercury is heated from 25°C to 155°C, and absorbs 455 joules of heat in the process. Calculate the specific heat capacity of mercury.
  6. To what temperature will a 50.0 g piece of glass raise if it absorbs 5275 joules of heat and its specific heat capacity is 0.50 J/g°C? The initial temperature of the glass is 20.0°C.
  7. What is the specific heat capacity of silver metal if 55.00 g of the metal absorbs 47.3 calories (change to Joules) of heat and the temperature rises 15.0°C?
  8. If a sample of chloroform is initially at 25°C, what is its final temperature if 150.0 g of chloroform absorbs 1.1 kilojoules of heat, and the specific heat of chloroform is 0.96 J/g°C?
  9. How many joules of heat are needed to raise the temperature of 30.0 g of aluminum from 23°C to 67°C, if the specific heat of aluminum is 0.90 J/g°C?
  10. To what temperature will a 40.0 g piece of gold raise if it absorbs 6,432 joules of heat and its specific heat capacity is 0.13 J/g°C? The initial temperature of the gold is 20.0°C.
